# Post-Production

## Editing and Refinement

Post-production was where LUCID became fully shaped as a film. This stage focused on editing, pacing, visual consistency, and strengthening the emotional tone of the project.

## Main Areas of Work

- assembling and refining the edit
- building suspense through rhythm and timing
- improving scene transitions
- shaping the sound and atmosphere
- refining the image through color and visual polish

## Workflow

The post-production process involved reviewing footage, experimenting with structure, and making decisions about how long moments should hold, when tension should build, and how the film should feel overall.

## Key Focus

One of the main focuses in post-production was making the film feel cohesive. Because the story relies on mood and uncertainty, the edit and overall presentation had to support that feeling from beginning to end.

## What I Learned

This stage taught me a lot about how much of a film’s identity is created after production. Editing, sound, and color were essential in turning the footage into a finished piece with a clear tone and stronger emotional impact.
