# Pre-Production

## Planning

Pre-production involved organizing the film’s visual direction, locations, and overall production strategy. I used this stage to define the look of the film and prepare for efficient shoot days.

## Key Areas of Preparation

- refining the concept and tone
- planning scenes and visual coverage
- identifying locations
- preparing camera and gear choices
- coordinating talent and logistics
- thinking through transitions, pacing, and how scenes would connect in the edit

## Visual Strategy

Because LUCID depends heavily on atmosphere, pre-production was important for making sure the visual language stayed consistent. I thought carefully about how camera movement, framing, and location choice could support suspense and unreality.

## Challenges

A major challenge in pre-production was balancing ambition with what was realistic to execute. Since the project needed to be both cinematic and achievable, I had to make practical decisions while protecting the core vision of the film.

## Outcome

This stage gave the project a stronger foundation and made production more intentional. It also helped me think ahead about how the footage would come together in post-production.
